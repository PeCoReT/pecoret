{{ _('Critical') }}

{% trans %} The risk rating "critical" represents the highest level of risk associated with a security issue. It indicates that the finding has the potential to cause severe damage, compromise sensitive data, or enable unauthorized access to critical systems or resources. The finding has a high likelihood of being exploited, either by a skilled attacker or through automated tools. The finding requires immediate attention and remediation to prevent potential breaches or compromise of critical assets. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('High') }}

{% trans %} The risk "high" describes a security issue that poses a significant threat to asset being tested. A high risk finding is easily exploitable, has a wide range of potential impacts, and may lead to unauthorized access, data breaches or system compromise. High risk findings may have limitations in exploitation or impact compared to findings classified as critical. The finding requires immediate attention and remediation to prevent potential breaches or compromise of critical assets. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Medium') }}

{% trans %} The risk rating "medium" indicates a level of risk that is not as severe as "high" but is more significant than "low". It suggests that there are weaknesses identified during the penetration testing that could potentially be exploited by attackers. A medium risk rating implies that the identified issues can pose a moderate threat to the security of the system or network. While they may not immediately result in a significant impact, they still have the potential to be exploited with some effort or under certain circumstances. However, the risk is not severe enough to cause immediate damage or compromise critical assets. Once identified, these findings should be addressed and mitigated by the organization as part of their risk management strategy. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Low') }}

{% trans %} This rating suggests that the issue, if exploited, is unlikely to result in significant harm or have a substantial impact on the target system or organization. The finding may have certain weaknesses or weaknesses that are difficult to exploit, significantly reducing the chances of successful exploitation. The consequences of exploiting the finding are minimal and generally do not pose a severe threat to the target organization's infrastructure, data, or operations. While a "low" risk rating may suggest a relatively low priority for immediate mitigation, it is essential to remember that maintaining a holistic security posture requires addressing findings, even if they present lower risks, to avoid potential future exploitation or connection with higher-risk findings. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Informational') }}

{% trans %} The risk rating "informational" refers a finding that poses little to no immediate threat or risk to the organization's systems or infrastructure. It means that the identified issue may not have a significant impact on the security of the environment, but it is still documented for informational purposes. An informational risk rating denotes findings that do not necessarily require immediate action or remediation but may provide valuable insights for improving the overall security posture. These findings could include things like harmless misconfigurations, or general information about the system's architecture. While Informational issues may not pose an immediate risk, they are still crucial for organizations to be aware of, as they provide an opportunity to proactively address potential security weaknesses before they can be exploited by malicious actors. Hence, it is essential to review and prioritize these findings accordingly, even if immediate remediation is not necessary. {% endtrans %}
